\section{Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure}
\label{sec:confidentiality}

\subsection{Definition of Confidential Information}
\label{subsec:confidential-definition}

\defterm{Confidential Information} means any and all information, whether written, oral,
electronic, or visual, disclosed by one Party (the \defterm{Disclosing Party}) to the
other Party (the \defterm{Receiving Party}) in connection with this Agreement, including
but not limited to:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Customer Information:} Customer names, contact information, property
          addresses, claim histories, preferences, and any other customer data;

    \item \textbf{Business Methods:} Sales techniques, marketing strategies, business
          processes, operational procedures, and training materials;

    \item \textbf{Financial Information:} Pricing structures, profit margins, revenue
          data, compensation arrangements, and financial performance information;

    \item \textbf{Proprietary Techniques:} Insurance supplement preparation methods,
          negotiation strategies, claim documentation techniques, and industry expertise;

    \item \textbf{Trade Secrets:} Any information that derives economic value from not
          being generally known and is subject to reasonable efforts to maintain its secrecy;

    \item \textbf{Business Relationships:} Information about vendors, suppliers, insurance
          adjusters, insurance carriers, and other business relationships;

    \item \textbf{Strategic Information:} Business plans, expansion strategies, competitive
          analyses, and other strategic business information.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Exclusions from Confidential Information}
\label{subsec:confidential-exclusions}

Confidential Information does not include information that:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Was publicly known at the time of disclosure or becomes publicly known through
          no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;

    \item Was rightfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing
          Party, as evidenced by written records;

    \item Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to
          the Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records;

    \item Is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without breach
          of any confidentiality obligation;

    \item Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order (provided the
          Receiving Party gives prompt notice to the Disclosing Party before such disclosure).
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Non-Disclosure Obligations}
\label{subsec:nondisclosure-obligations}

\subsubsection{Duty of Confidentiality}
The Receiving Party agrees to:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Maintain the confidentiality of all Confidential Information received from the
          Disclosing Party;

    \item Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written
          consent of the Disclosing Party;

    \item Use the Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing obligations
          or exercising rights under this Agreement;

    \item Protect Confidential Information with the same degree of care used to protect its
          own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care;

    \item Limit access to Confidential Information to employees, contractors, or advisors
          who have a legitimate need to know and who are bound by confidentiality obligations
          at least as protective as those in this Agreement.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Prohibited Uses}
The Receiving Party shall not:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Use Confidential Information to compete with the Disclosing Party;
    \item Disclose Confidential Information to competitors of the Disclosing Party;
    \item Use Confidential Information for any purpose other than performing under this Agreement;
    \item Reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble any Confidential Information;
    \item Remove or alter any confidentiality notices or proprietary markings.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Duration of Confidentiality Obligation}
\label{subsec:confidentiality-duration}

The obligations of confidentiality and non-disclosure set forth in this Section shall:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Commence on the Effective Date of this Agreement;
    \item Continue during the term of this Agreement;
    \item Survive termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of \textbf{five (5) years};
    \item Continue indefinitely for any Confidential Information that constitutes a trade
          secret under applicable law.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Return or Destruction of Confidential Information}
\label{subsec:return-confidential}

Upon termination of this Agreement or upon request by the Disclosing Party at any time,
the Receiving Party shall:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Promptly return to the Disclosing Party all documents, materials, and other
          tangible items containing or reflecting Confidential Information;

    \item Permanently delete or destroy all electronic copies of Confidential Information
          in the Receiving Party's possession or control;

    \item Provide written certification to the Disclosing Party that all Confidential
          Information has been returned or destroyed;

    \item Except: The Receiving Party may retain one copy of Confidential Information solely
          for archival purposes and to the extent required by law or professional regulations,
          subject to continuing confidentiality obligations.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Customer and Business Relationships}
\label{subsec:customer-relationships}

\subsubsection{Customer Information Protection}
The Parties acknowledge that customer information is highly sensitive and valuable.
Each Party specifically agrees:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Not to use customer contact information for purposes outside this Agreement;
    \item Not to solicit customers of the other Party for competing services during the
          term of this Agreement and for twelve (12) months after termination;
    \item To maintain customer information in secure systems with appropriate access controls;
    \item To comply with all applicable privacy laws and regulations regarding customer data.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Insurance Carrier Relationships}
Information regarding relationships with insurance carriers, adjusters, and other industry
contacts shall be treated as highly confidential and shall not be used to circumvent or
compete with the other Party.

\subsection{Remedies for Breach}
\label{subsec:confidentiality-remedies}

\subsubsection{Acknowledgment of Harm}
The Parties acknowledge that breach of confidentiality obligations would cause irreparable
harm for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy.

\subsubsection{Equitable Relief}
In the event of a breach or threatened breach of confidentiality obligations, the
Disclosing Party shall be entitled to:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Seek immediate injunctive relief and specific performance without the necessity
          of posting a bond;
    \item Recover all damages caused by the breach;
    \item Recover all costs and attorneys' fees incurred in enforcing this Section;
    \item Pursue any other remedies available at law or in equity.
\end{itemize}

The right to seek injunctive relief shall be in addition to, and not in lieu of, any
other remedies available to the Disclosing Party.
